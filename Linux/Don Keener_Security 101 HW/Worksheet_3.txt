Cybersecurity Threat Landscape (Part 3 - Verizon)

1. The difference between an incident and a breach is as follows.  An incident is a security event that compromises the integrity,confidentiality or avialability of an information asset.  A breach is an incident that results in the confirmed disclosure, not just the potential exposure, of data to an unauthorized party.

2. Around 80% of the breaches were external and around 20% were internal.

3. Around 80% of the breaches were perpetrated by organized criminal groups.

4. Over 75% of the breaches wer financialy motivated.

5. Denial of Service: is a cyber-attack in which the perpetrator seeks to make a machine or network resource unavailable to its intended users by temporarily or indefinitely disrupting services of a host connected to the Internet.
   
   Command and Control: is a computer controlled by an attacker or cybercriminal which is used to send commands to systems compromised by malware and receive stolen data from a target network. 

   Backdoor: A backdoor is a typically covert method of bypassing normal authentication or encryption in a computer.

   Keylogger: Keyloggers are a type of monitoring software designed to record keystrokes made by a user.
